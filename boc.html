BASICS OF CYBERSECURITY

EXPLAINED !

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity involves protecting computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks and theft. It encompasses technologies, processes and practices designed to secure information from unauthorized access or harm.

Key principles of Cybersecurity

  1. Confidentiality
  2. Integrity
  3. Availability
  4. Malware prevention
  5. Risk Management

Importance of Cyber Hygiene:

  1. Use of Strong Passwords: A combination of letters, numbers, and special characters.
  2. Update Software Regularly: Keep systems and applications updated to protect against known vulnerabilities
  3. Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Adds an extra layer of protection beyond just a password

Common Cyber Threats:

  • Phishing Attacks: Deception emails or messages designed to trick users into providing sensitive information. Real-life case: In 2016, a phishing email targeted employess at the Democratic National Committee (DNC), leading to a major email leak.
  • Ransonware: Malicious software that can damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems. Real-life case: The WannaCry ransomware attack in 2017 affected over 200,000 computers in 150 countries, crippling healthcare and other services.
  • Data Breaches: Unauthorized access to confidential data. Real-life case: In 2023, a major breach at T-Mobile exposed the data of 37 million customers.

Why Cybersecurity Matters?

Cyber attacks can affect everyone from individuals to global corporations. Understanding the basics of cybersecurity helps protect personal information, financial assets, and even national security.
